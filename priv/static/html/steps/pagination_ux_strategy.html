UX Strategy for Pagination and Real-Time Updates

General Principles

  1. Keeping Key Items Always in View
    Newly added, edited, or deleted items remain visible in the current view, even if they don’t naturally belong to the page based on sorting or pagination. This ensures users always see immediate feedback for their actions, maintaining trust and a consistent interface. Deleted items are not removed abruptly but marked visually until the user navigates away or refreshes.

  2. Handling Simultaneous User Actions

    • If an item is modified by another user after the form is opened, saving displays an error: “This language has been modified by someone else.”
    • If an item is being edited, it cannot be deleted by another user. Attempting to delete shows the error: “Cannot delete language while it is being edited by another user.”

Additions

  • When a new item is added, it is temporarily placed at the top of the list, regardless of its actual sorted position.
  • The new item is highlighted with a green background and a “New” badge. These indicators remain until the user navigates away, refreshes, or reloads the page to sort the item into its correct position.
  • The user who adds the item sees: “Item created successfully. It has been temporarily added to the top of the list. [Click here to reload and sort now].”
  • Other users see: “A new item has been added to the list by another user. [Click here to reload and sort now].”

Edits

  • Edited items are updated in place and remain in their current position until reset or navigation.
  • Edited items are highlighted with a blue background and marked with an “Edited” badge for easy identification.
  • The user who edits the item sees: “Item updated successfully. [Click here to reload and sort now].”
  • Other users see: “An item has been updated by another user. [Click here to reload and sort now].”

Deletions

  • Deleted items remain visible but are marked with a gray background, a strikethrough, and a “Deleted” badge.
  • Items are only fully removed when the user navigates away or refreshes the page.
  • The user who deletes the item sees: “Item deleted. It will be removed from the list when you navigate away or refresh. [Click here to reload and sort now].”
  • Other users see: “An item has been deleted by another user. [Click here to reload and sort now].”

Summary

  • The summary (e.g., "1 - 6 of 101") reflects the visible rows and total count.
  • After an addition, the summary updates immediately to reflect the new total count. For example:
    • Before addition: “1 - 5 of 100”
    • After addition: “1 - 6 of 101”
  • After a deletion, the summary remains gray to indicate that it is temporarily out of sync with the actual total. For example:
    • Before deletion: “1 - 10 of 100”
    • After deletion: “1 - 10 of 100” (with gray text)

Pagination

  • Pagination does not immediately reflect changes caused by additions or deletions, avoiding disruptive updates during interaction.
  • Pagination updates only upon navigation or a full page refresh, at which point all changes are fully synchronized.
  • Example scenarios:
    • If a new item is added and the total exceeds the current page limit, the pagination component remains unchanged until the user navigates or refreshes.
    • If all items on the current page are deleted, navigating to the next page recalculates and adjusts the pagination to reflect the new total.

This strategy ensures a seamless user experience, balancing real-time responsiveness, clarity of feedback, and stability of the interface. It accounts for both individual and collaborative actions in a multi-user environment.
